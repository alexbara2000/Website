I moved to Canada at the age of 5. I did not know the language and I had no friends. It was a difficult time for me and it was hard to integrate with other people. When I was at school, I could not wait for the day to be done so I could go home and talk in a language I actually understood. Slowly but surely I started to undestand and learn french. I went to a french school, Katimavik Hebert which is where I started to grow a passion for math since it was one of the only classes I actually understood what was going on.

Katimavik Hebert, my primary school

College Sainte-Anne, my high school



In high school, I was comfortable with the language french. I would speak to everyone in french. All my friends would also speak french. But in secondary 4, everything changed. All my class was speaking english. So slowly I started to adapt and speak english to them. I discovered the beauty and simplicity of english and from them I never stopped loving english. It became my go to language in every day conversation pretty quickly. I also joined a science club at Sainte Anne. It was in the science club that I discovered programming. It felt like an universal language with unlimited possibilities. So I started to learn different languages and became fascinated by the logic and math behind it.
